Facts About the Colosseum


  • The Colosseum was built by thousands of slaves under the rule of Emperor Vespasian between 72 AD and 80 AD.
  • Entry to the Colosseum was free and paid for by the emperor.
  • Some of the events in the Colosseum could last for 100 days.
  • A variety of animals from all over the Roman Empire were brought to fight including lions, tigers, bears and crocodiles.
  • The largest amphitheater in the world, the Colosseum could seat 50,000 people.
  • During certain events the Colosseum was flooded with water to create mock sea battles.
