Why should you visit?


The largest elliptical amphitheater ever built the Colosseum is one of the most famous landmarks in Rome and in the world. Though the Colosseum is almost two thousand years old and has been earthquakes, fires and stone-robbers it is still incredibly well preserved. The Colosseum is one of the most visited places in Rome. In 1980 it became a UNESCO World Heritage site and in 2001 it was voted as one of the New Seven Wonders of the world. With the underground portion of the Colosseum recently being open to the public there has never been a better time to visit. Today visitors can go back in time and tour the Colosseum on their own or by taking a guided tour.


Colosseum Tours


There are multiple ways you can tour the Colosseum. You can tour the structure on your own with an audio guide that will give you the information you need to get around and learn some interesting facts. It is highly recommended however that you book a group tour as this will allow you to skip the line and tour guides will often have access to restricted parts of the Colosseum.
